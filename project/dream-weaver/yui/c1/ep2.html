-Yui-If mom's home, tell her I'm going to be late. They're making me stay.

-Yui-Sorry =^^;=

-Yui's brother-I'm sorry to hear that. You're not taking the blame for something you didn't do again, are you?

Just as I started to write my reply to my brother a hand reached out and grabbed my phone.

ClassmateHey! Who's oniichan!?

YuiM-my brother. Give it back!

ClassmateYou know you're supposed to be cleaning, not chatting with your boyfriend.

YuiIt's really my brother.

ClassmateI guess that's more likely. Who'd actually like you!?

ClassmateYou know what? I'm just going to hold on to this until you're done.

YuiWhy are you always picking on me?

ClassmateBecause its fun.

ClassmateOh, are you going to cry?

ClassmateLet's see those tears!

The girl held on to my phone and watched me as I spent the rest of the afternoon cleaning a classroom that wasn't even mine with no help at all.

I didn't give her the satisfaction of crying. I just focused on my work.

As the sun started going down, she got up and tossed my phone. I managed to catch it.

-Classmate-See you tomorrow, Yui.

I checked my phone. She didn't send anything to my brother. I sighed in relief.

Since transferring to Seven Hearts Academy, I was told to never to stay on campus after dark.

I hurried to my shoe locker.

I prayed no one had done anything to my outdoor shoes.

I quickly opened the locker.

Everything seemed fine, so I took my outdoor shoes out and slipped my indoor shoes into the locker.

Something crawled out of one of my shoes.

I screamed.

As I took both shoes and beat them against the lockers to make sure nothing else was in them, I quietly wished all of this would just end.

I was happy the school day was finally over, but I couldn't relax.

I hadn't checked my phone since I got it back, and I hadn't ever replied to my brother, so I didn't know if mom was home.

If she wasn't maybe the night wouldn't be too bad.

I could take a bath, change into my pyjamas and go to bed.

Maybe, if I was lucky, I wouldn't even wake up in the morning.

Before I even got to the door I could hear yelling.

Mom was home.

-Yui's brother-Do you even know what she goes through at school!?

-Yui's brother-Do you even care!?

-Yui's mother-How dare you!?

-Yui's mother-How dare I what? Tell you your daughter cries herself to sleep every night, or that it's your fault.

-Yui-Stop it!

I shouted!

I couldn't believe I had shouted.

I didn't even know why. Just that I didn't want anyone fighting because of me.

...

Even though I shouted, no one seemed to hear me...

they didn't stop.

-Yui's mother-I'm trying my best.

-Yui's brother-Your best? How much have you had to drink tonight?

-Yui's mother-Shut the hell up!

-Yui's brother-Sounds like a plan. Rather than just face the problems we have and talk about them like adults we'll just bury them and they'll go away!

-Yui's brother-Well, guess what?

-Yui's brother-Yui is hurting and that hurt isn't going away!

-Yui's mother-She's hurting!?

-Yui's mother-Yeah, we lost dad too you know, but, you have a good job, and I'm doing okay at school...

On one hand, it felt kind of nice for my brother to be defending me, but...

I just couldn't help but think I wasn't really worth that kind of effort.

It just made me want to fade away even more.

I wrapped my arm around my brother's waist.

-Yui-Please stop fighting!

-Yui's brother-Yui!?

-Yui's mother-Go to your room!
