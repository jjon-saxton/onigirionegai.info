Yui's house, front, evening

Caption (Yui)

At home, I had a kind older half-brother, an understanding father, and a mother who always seemed to know just what to say.

Whether I spoke German or Japanese, everyone at home always seemed to know what I meant to say, even if I didn't use the right words, or the right tone. They could always tell.

At home, I was happy. I didn't have to worry about what was going to happen. I didn't have to worry about anything. All father expected was that I help with dinner and dishes. Mother just wanted me to put my own laundry in the machine each Sunday and do my homework each night. My brother just wanted to talk or play games with me.

That's just how it was until one night when my father didn't come home. . .
